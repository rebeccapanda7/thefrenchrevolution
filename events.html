Vocabulary

King

Louis XVI was king of France during this time. Although it was during the time of absolute monarchs, Louis XVI was easily manipulated by the noble judges of parement in Paris. The nobles and clergy asserted their power over the king and weakened the monarch.

First Estate

Clergy- church officials

Second Estate

Nobility- land owners

Third Estate

Everyone Else- peasants, merchants, commoners, etc.

Causes

The first and second estate consisted of around 3% of the population, with only the clergy and nobility. However, the third estate consisted of the rest of the population: peasants, lower class, merchants, etc.
Prior to King Louis XVI's reign in France. Former King Louis XIV put France into an economic crisis. Louis XIV fought endless wars to assert dominance and to gain territory for France. However, those wars were expensive and threw France into a lot of debt. Taxes were then implemented for majority of the citizens of France, excluding the clergy and nobility, meaning that they were exempt from the economic burden, which was then placed on the already struggling commoners.
Louis XVI called an Estates-General meeting to get approval for raising taxes because the French Parlement shut him down. Each estate only had one vote, meaning three votes in total with majority winning. However, because the clergy and nobility had similar interests because they were a part of the upper class, both the first and second estate voted against the third estate. Because of this, the third estate never got fair representation. The people of France wanted an equal seat at the table of power.
There were bread shortages throughout the country, and 97% of the population were going hungry. They brought this problem up to the first and second estate. However, the clergy and nobility had made no action to help the rest of the population because it did not affect them.

June 17, 1789-

National Assembly

The 3rd estate decides that they have had enough of this inequality, so they left the Estates General and declared themselves as the true representative body of France, calling themselves the National Assembly. They granted themselves exempt from all taxes. However, at the next Estates General meeting, they were barred from getting in.

August 14th, 1789-

Storming of the Bastille

This was the first real act of the revolution. Louis XVI was secretly assembling troops to crush the National Assembly. However, this plot was discovered, and a rebel group call the San-Culottes stormed the Bastille. Members of the National Assembly as well as many other commoner citizens stormed the Bastille. The Bastille was a armory and a prison with prisoners who were against Louis XVI. They freed all the prisoners and stole weapons and armor. They needed protection from the soldiers. They also took the warden hostage, who was then later killed in the aftermath. They people cut off the heads of the soldiers and put them on pitchforks as a sign not to mess with them as they walked through Paris. King Louis XVI got really concerned about the riots and political opposition.
